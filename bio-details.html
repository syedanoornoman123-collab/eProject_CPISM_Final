Course Overview

This advanced course delves into the fascinating world of genetics and molecular biology, exploring the fundamental mechanisms that govern life at the molecular level. You'll journey from DNA structure and replication to gene expression, regulation, and the latest advancements in genetic engineering and biotechnology.

Through comprehensive lectures, virtual laboratory simulations, and case studies, you'll gain practical insights into modern molecular techniques and their applications in medicine, agriculture, and research. This course is designed for students pursuing careers in biomedical sciences, genetic counseling, or biotechnology.

What You'll Learn

  • Master DNA structure, replication, and repair mechanisms
  • Understand gene expression, transcription, and translation
  • Explore genetic regulation in prokaryotes and eukaryotes
  • Analyze Mendelian and molecular genetics principles
  • Apply recombinant DNA technology and genetic engineering
  • Understand genomics, proteomics, and bioinformatics
  • Study genetic disorders and molecular diagnostics
  • Explore CRISPR and gene editing technologies
  • Analyze molecular evolution and phylogenetic relationships
  • Develop laboratory skills in molecular biology techniques

Prerequisites

This advanced-level course requires solid foundation in basic biology concepts including cell structure, metabolism, and introductory genetics. Familiarity with basic chemistry principles is recommended for understanding molecular interactions and biochemical pathways.

Students should have completed introductory biology courses or have equivalent knowledge. The course includes supplementary materials covering foundational concepts for those who need to refresh their basic biology knowledge before tackling advanced molecular biology topics.
